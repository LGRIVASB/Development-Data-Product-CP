Swiss Fertility and Socioeconomic Indicators (1888) Data

Standardized fertility measure and socio-economic indicators for each of 47 French-speaking provinces of Switzerland at about 1888.

Format

A data frame with 47 observations and 6 variables each of which is in percent, i.e., in [0, 100]. Note: All variables but ‘Fertility’ give proportions of the population.

  • Fertility, Ig, ‘common standardized fertility measure’
  • Agriculture, % of males involved in agriculture as occupation
  • Examination, % draftees receiving highest mark on army examination
  • Education, % education beyond primary school for draftees.
  • Catholic, % ‘catholic’ (as opposed to ‘protestant’).
  • Infant.Mortality, live births who live less than 1 year.
